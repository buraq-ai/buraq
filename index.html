Insights

Discover our system's role in protecting global human rights.

How does the system work offline?

Buraq AI uses a lightweight, field-adapted Kubernetes cluster architecture to maintain functionality in offline or intermittently connected environments. Each edge deployment runs on compact compute units configured as nodes within a self-contained Kubernetes cluster. This enables continuous monitoring, even in fully disconnected or jammed zones.

Why is the data legally valid?

Buraq AI aligns all evidence collection with international standards, such as the Rome Statute, Universal Declaration of Human Rights, UN Chain of Custody Protocols, and the Geneva Conventions.

Is it effective in conflict areas?

Yes—Buraq AI is specifically engineered for deployment in conflict zones where traditional monitoring systems fail. Effectiveness is achieved through offline autonomy, portability, real-time documentation, and resilience.

Who benefits from this technology?

Buraq AI benefits legal investigators, humanitarian organizations, journalists, and conflict-affected communities by providing real-time, verifiable documentation of human rights violations. It enables safer monitoring, stronger legal cases, and faster international response—even in areas inaccessible to humans. From the ICC to frontline NGOs, Buraq AI empowers those fighting for truth and accountability.
